The `src/reconic` package is installed as the `reconic` PyPI distribution (setup.py entry points `reconic-train`, `reconic-eval`) and exposes a top-level `__init__.py` re-exporting `datasets`, `engines`, `models`, `pipelines`, `trainers`, `utils`. Cross-child wiring follows a layered contract:
- `training_loop` orchestrators (`GenerativeReconTrainingLoop`, `ReconTrainingLoop`) own the training schedule, instantiate a `trainers` trainer plus an `engines` scheduler/engine pair via `import_str(cfg.generative_engine.scheduler/type)`, and periodically feed rendered RGB + ego masks from the trainer into the generative engine.
- `datasets.base.data_proto` (`CameraInfo`, `ImageInfo`) is the shared protobuf-like tensor protocol consumed by both `trainers` and `simulator`/`engines` so novel-view data flows between reconstruction and generation without coupling.
- `models` provides the scene representation primitives (Gaussian splatting backends, SMPL human body, diffusion fixers) used by `trainers` and `pipelines`; `pipelines` wraps Stable Diffusion UNet/DiFix pipelines invoked by the generative engine.
- `simulator` is an offline renderer driven by `scripts_and_deploy` shell wrappers; it consumes trained Gaussians from `models` and writes novel views that `multi_vehicle_utils` later packages into DDS recordings.
- `cli_and_metrics` CLI commands dispatch to the same `training_loop` / `trainer` stack, while `metrics` evaluates detection on rendered outputs against projected 3D GT.
The joint-training loop (`generative_recon_training_loop.py`) is the single place where these layers are bound together: it alternates reconstruction steps with generative-engine inference/training, persists per-step checkpoints under `project_dir`, and coordinates novel-view cache directories.
